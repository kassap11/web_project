BodyBuilding

  1. Resistance Training

    Weightlifting: Focus on compound exercises (e.g., squats, deadlifts, bench press)

  2. Cardiovascular Exercise

    High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): Short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or lower-intensity exercise to improve cardiovascular health and burn fat

  3. Rest and Recovery

    Adequate Sleep: Essential for muscle recovery, hormone regulation, and overall well-being. Rest Days: Scheduled breaks to allow muscles to recover and prevent overtraining.

powerlifter

  1. Squat:

    High Bar Squat or Low Bar Squat Box Squats

  2. Bench Press:

    Competition Bench Press Paused Bench Press

  3. Deadlift:

    Conventional Deadlift or Sumo Deadlift

  4. Record Keeping:

    Training Logs: Keeping detailed records of training sessions, including weights lifted, sets, and repetitions.

cross-fit

  1. Box Jumps and Plyometrics:

    Activities such as box jumps, burpees, and other plyometric exercises are used to develop explosive power and agility.

  2. Functional Movements:

    CrossFit emphasizes functional movements that mimic real-life activities. These include squats, deadlifts, presses, pulls, and various bodyweight exercises

  3. Functional Training Equipment:

    CrossFit workouts utilize various equipment such as kettlebells, medicine balls, resistance bands, and plyometric boxes to create diverse and challenging exercises
